Palm Beach Parking Permits 2026

Town of Palm Beach — Permit Types, Costs & Application Process
Approach

Before beginning any application, I would confirm with the principal: the property address on the island (determines eligibility: RPP vs. PPP); the number of vehicles that need permits (maximum 2 per household); whether visitor hangtags are needed and for how many guests (up to 4 per RPP household); and any commercial or staff vehicles that may require a Parking Placard Permit. This prevents wasted effort and ensures the right permit type is applied for from the start.

Permit Types & Costs

Resident Parking Permit (RPP)
Who It's For
Residents on qualifying street blocks
$50 / vehicle
Key Benefit
Park on your designated block; up to 48 hrs
ParkMobile Parking Permit (PPP)
Who It's For
All Town residents
$50 / vehicle
Key Benefit
2 free hours in any ParkMobile zone town-wide
RPP Visitor Hangtag
Who It's For
Guests of RPP-eligible residents
$50 / hangtag
Key Benefit
Park on resident's RPP block; up to 4 per household
Parking Placard Permit
Who It's For
Employers / commercial users
$165 / mo  ·  $554 / 4 mo  ·  $1,454 / yr
Key Benefit
Unlimited parking in designated commercial areas; waitlist may apply
Key Rules
Maximum of 2 permits per household for both RPP and PPP. All resident permits are valid through December 31, 2026. RPP residents may add PPP for an additional $50, or purchase PPP on its own.

Documents to Gather Before Applying

Proof of Residency
One required
Current utility bill; court-registered mortgage document, deed, condominium contract, or trust document; current Florida ID or Driver's License showing Town address; or current lease agreement (must include landlord's name and contact number). Note: hotels and short-term rentals under 12 months are not eligible.
Vehicle Information
All required
Make and model; license plate number (vehicle does not need a Florida plate).
Vehicle Document
One required
Car registration, insurance card, or rental contract.

How to Apply — Online (Recommended)

The Town uses the Gtechna parking permit portal, which launched for 2026 registrations on January 26, 2026.

1
Create an account

Navigate to the Palm Beach Permit Portal, click "Sign Up" in the top right. The system automatically determines your eligibility (RPP or PPP) based on your address.

2
Register your vehicle

Click "Add Permit," then "Add Vehicle," enter vehicle details, and upload your Vehicle Registration and Proof of Residency.

3
Select your permit type

RPP residents: location defaults to your block. PPP residents: select "Townwide ParkMobile Parking Permit" from the dropdown.

4
Add visitor hangtags (optional — RPP residents only)

Click "Add Permit," then "Add Vehicle," toggle "Is this a visitor vehicle?" to Yes. Use format: VIS + last 5 digits of your phone number + permit number (e.g., VIS434561).

5
Pay and submit

Click "Add Credit Card," enter card details, accept terms, and click "Buy." Your card is not charged until the application is approved. Confirmation arrives by email.

In-Person Alternative

Applications can also be submitted in person at Town Hall. Bring all documents listed above. Staff will process on-site and issue the permit same day in most cases.

Online Survey

Microsoft Forms + Power Automate — 150 to 200 Respondents
Recommendation

The recommended platform is Microsoft Forms, which integrates natively with the M365 ecosystem and lets board members respond without creating an account or logging in. Each submission triggers an automated workflow that generates a clean, one-page PDF summary of the full response and delivers it by email, eliminating any manual export or formatting work.

Tool Stack
Microsoft Forms (collection) → Word template on OneDrive (one-page response layout) → Power Automate (populate template, convert to PDF, send email) → Outlook (internal email delivery)

How the Workflow Runs

1
Collection — Microsoft Forms

Respondent submits via a shared form link. No account or login required. Responses are captured instantly and stored in the connected M365 environment.

2
Layout — Word Template on OneDrive

Each answer is mapped into a structured Word template stored in OneDrive, producing a consistent one-page "Survey Response" layout per submission.

3
Automation — Power Automate

Power Automate converts the completed template to PDF and emails it to the designated internal recipient inbox on a per-response basis. Summaries arrive within minutes of completion and are easy to forward or archive.

4
Delivery — Outlook

PDF summary lands in the designated inbox. For teams that prefer less frequent notifications, the workflow can be configured to send consolidated summaries weekly or every two weeks instead of in real time.

Why Microsoft Forms
The Case for Owl Capital

The survey is intended to run fully inside the existing Microsoft 365 environment, which makes Microsoft Forms and Power Automate the most natural fit. This stack minimizes additional vendors and permissions, keeps data within the firm's tenant, and leverages tools the team already uses daily — while still delivering an automated, one-page PDF summary workflow that can be extended or maintained easily over time.

Outlook Contacts

Contact Consolidation — PowerShell Detection + Excel Merge + Outlook Import
Approach

I would use PowerShell as a control tool, not as the consolidation tool itself. Its role is to scan the exported Outlook contacts, normalize the data, identify likely duplicates, and produce a review report. This gives me a reliable way to locate duplicate risk across a large contact list without touching the original Outlook data. The actual consolidation would happen in Excel, where I can manually validate each flagged group, preserve all useful contact details, and produce one clean master CSV for import back into Outlook.

Tool Stack
Outlook (export) → Raw CSV Backup → PowerShell (duplicate detection) → Duplicate_Report.csv (reference only) → Excel Working File (full dataset) → Controlled Merge → Contacts_CLEAN.csv → Outlook (import)

Process Flow

1
Outlook Export

Export the full contacts list from Outlook via File → Open & Export → Import/Export → Export to a file → Comma Separated Values. This is the raw source — untouched.

2
Raw CSV Backup

Save a locked copy of the original export before any processing begins. This is the fallback if anything goes wrong downstream.

3
PowerShell Duplicate Detection

New Outlook may automatically hide exact or subset duplicates, but this process does not rely on hidden duplicate behavior. The full export is reviewed and consolidated before re-import. Run a PowerShell script to normalize name and email fields, flag likely duplicates based on matching criteria, and output a structured report. No records are modified at this stage.

4
Duplicate_Report.csv — Reference Only

Review the flagged groups. This file is for review only — no edits are made here. Each flagged group is assessed to confirm whether it's a true duplicate or a legitimate separate contact.

5
Excel Working File — Full Dataset

Open the full export in Excel. Use the Duplicate Report as a reference to manually validate each flagged group, preserve all useful contact details, and consolidate confirmed duplicates into single clean records.

6
Controlled Merge

Each confirmed contact is reviewed for completeness, including name, company, title, phone, mobile, email, notes, and category where available. Contacts without a verified email or phone are flagged for follow-up before inclusion.

7
Contacts_CLEAN.csv

Export the finalized dataset as a clean CSV — formatted and mapped to Outlook's import field schema. This is the only file that touches Outlook on re-import.

8
Outlook Import

Import via File → Open & Export → Import/Export → Import from another program or file → Comma Separated Values → map fields → Import. Contacts populate in the default Contacts folder under their assigned category.
